What's an Open Innovation Tournament? 🤔

An Open Innovation Tournament is a dynamic and collaborative event where individuals and teams come together to generate, develop, and showcase innovative ideas. Unlike traditional innovation processes that often occur within closed environments, an Open Innovation Tournament invites participants from diverse backgrounds—such as entrepreneurs, intrapreneurs, policymakers, and makers—to contribute their unique perspectives and skills.

Participants engage in various activities like brainstorming sessions, prototyping, and pitching ideas, all aimed at solving real-world challenges. The tournament fosters a competitive yet cooperative atmosphere, encouraging participants to push the boundaries of creativity and problem-solving. By the end of the event, the most promising solutions are recognized and rewarded, with the potential for further development and implementation.

The key benefits of an Open Innovation Tournament include:

  • Diverse Perspectives: Bringing together a wide range of participants to inspire creative solutions.
  • Collaborative Environment: Encouraging teamwork and knowledge sharing.
  • Real-World Impact: Focusing on practical solutions to pressing challenges.
  • Networking Opportunities: Connecting innovators with potential partners, mentors, and investors.

How Will the Philly Innovation Tournament Work? 🛠️

The Philly Innovation Tournament is structured to systematically generate and evaluate innovative ideas through a competitive process inspired by the framework developed by Profs. Karl Ulrich and Christian Terwiesch (@UPenn in Philadelphia). Here's a brief overview of how the tournament will unfold:

Tournament Structure Overview

Participants will brainstorm and create rapid prototypes of their ideas, displaying them on poster boards for peer evaluation. The most promising concepts will advance through multiple rounds, where participants will refine their ideas and pitches. The tournament culminates in final presentations judged by experts, ensuring that the best ideas are identified and developed into actionable projects.

Watch this video for more information about the tournament setup from its creators.

Process

The tournament spans two days, featuring rapid prototyping sessions, peer voting, and expert judging. Participants will engage in brainstorming, prototyping, pitching, and evaluation rounds, competing for awards and recognition.

Day 1: Saturday

  • Morning: Doors open and the tournament kicks off with a rapid prototyping session. Teams brainstorm and create poster board presentations of their ideas.
  • Midday: Participants vote on the best ideas, and the top concepts (about the top 40%) advance. Losing teams will be invited to resubmit in a repechage round.
  • Afternoon: A session on delivering elevator pitches is followed by breakout sessions where teams create light pitches. The day ends with 3-minute elevator pitches and another round of voting, narrowing the field to no more than 10 top teams.

Day 2: Sunday

  • Our panel of judges (Local VCs, Angels and Innovation Leaders) listen to pitches from the final teams. The event concludes with awards for the best ideas, followed by support and assistance for promising teams to help them raise funds or support for their projects.
